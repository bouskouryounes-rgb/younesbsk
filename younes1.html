Moroccan Msemen

Flaky, golden layers of North African tradition

What is msemen?

Msemen (or Msammen) is a traditional Moroccan flatbread known for its flaky, layered texture and buttery flavor. It’s often served at breakfast or tea time, accompanied by honey, jam, or melted butter.

Traditional Msemen Recipe

ingredients

2 cups fine semolina

1 cup all-purpose flour

1 tea spoon of salt

1 cup of warm water

Oil and melted butter for folding

Preparation

Mix the semolina, flour, and salt. Gradually add water until a soft dough forms. Knead until smooth and elastic. Divide into balls, coat with oil, and let rest. Flatten each ball thinly, fold into a square, and cook on a hot skillet with a bit of oil until golden on both sides.

Cultural Significance

Msemen is more than food — it’s part of Moroccan hospitality. Families gather for breakfast around mint tea, freshly made msemen, and conversation. The rhythmic sound of folding and frying msemen is a familiar morning melody in Moroccan homes.
